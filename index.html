The_SAT

I was on the brink of selling my soul

SAT, short for Scholastic Assessment Test. It is a standardized test that you can use to apply for international universities. You can register for it on collegeboard.org which also offers study materials and practice tests in their KhanAcademy collab. There is plenty of free study material online and KhanAcademy SAT is the one I would recommend the most, however, the test itself is pretty expensive which would cost you more than $100, and aside from that test centers are pretty limited so you might have to travel someplace else for the test. So now with these expenses, my first advice is DON‚ÄôT THROW THE TEST.

If you plan on studying internationally, this is a test you can take or the ACT but I don‚Äôt know much about that one. It is really vital that if you plan to take, take it as soon as possible, preferably before school starts so that you can dedicate fully to preparing for it because I swear it will be hard to juggle between acads and studying for the SAT. The seats are also limited and would usually be full 2 months prior (like back in the December 2021 SAT).

As for studying for the test, I will not stop emphasizing making use of the KhanAcademy SAT. The practice tests there are also made to be similar to the actual ones. It would also benefit you to employ effective study techniques such as spaced repetition and active recall. Another is to focus more on the mastery of your strengths, getting the easy questions correct will benefit you more than struggling to find the answer to the harder questions because all of them are worth one point - even the questions with no choices in the Math sections.

I also would advise you not to grind and study 8 hours every day or you will burn yourself out before the exam (just like I did huhu) unless you‚Äôre a monster. Allot at least 50 days before the d-day to study. Lastly, there is also a lot of other reliable SAT prep material such as Barron‚Äôs or Erica Meltzer‚Äôs, the latter I highly recommend for reading comprehension (the hardest to perfect). Oh, and take the SAT essay especially if you are aiming for the top universities.

International_Uni_Apps

The Common App is your bestie

Common App is basically a site where a lot of universities have their online application procedures like Edukasyon.ph. Most universities from the United States and Europe are available through Common App (for Asia, you‚Äôd have to search up the portals yourself, sadly). I suggest you make an account early on and choose target schools, even ones that you only want to join in your dreams so that you can receive emails in case they offer application fee waivers or scholarships. You can then ask your school registrar to upload your Transcript of Records or Form 138 in Common App (they know what to do).

For international universities, it is really recommended that you apply for Early Decision because a lot say that you would have higher success rates. This is usually around in October or November so it would do you well to take the ACT or SAT (if required) earlier than that.

Again I didn‚Äôt really apply for International unis, I just took the SAT for clout (but don‚Äôt be like me, make most of everything).
